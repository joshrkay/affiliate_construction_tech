# Best Tax Reporting Software for Construction Contractors (2025)

*Note: This guide covers software tools for construction tax compliance. It is not tax advice. Work with a construction-specialized CPA for guidance on your specific tax situation.*

Construction tax compliance has layers that general business accounting software doesn't handle well. Certified payroll on prevailing wage jobs requires a WH-347 report submitted to the contracting agency every week — and it has to match your actual payroll records exactly. Every subcontractor you paid more than $600 in a calendar year needs a 1099-NEC by January 31. Sales tax on materials varies by state and project type in ways that catch contractors off guard.

Most construction companies manage this with QuickBooks, a spreadsheet for certified payroll, and a CPA who asks the same questions every March. The tools in this guide reduce the manual work, reduce the errors, and give your accountant cleaner data to work with.

## The Four Tax Compliance Problems Specific to Construction

### Certified Payroll on Prevailing Wage Jobs

Any federally funded project or state-funded project above a threshold requires Davis-Bacon Act (federal) or state prevailing wage compliance. You must pay workers the prevailing wage for their trade classification in that county, and you must submit a certified payroll report (federal Form WH-347 or equivalent) to the contracting agency every week for every worker on the job.

Getting this wrong triggers back pay liability — you owe the difference between what you paid and the prevailing wage rate, plus penalties. Getting it right manually is time-consuming: matching workers to correct trade classifications, verifying wage rates, and preparing the WH-347 each week.

### 1099-NEC Filing for Subcontractors

Every subcontractor or independent contractor you paid $600 or more during the calendar year requires a 1099-NEC filed with the IRS and sent to the sub by January 31. The common mistakes:

- **Cash payments to subs** that weren't tracked — if you paid a sub in cash and didn't keep records, you have a 1099 problem
- **Missing W-9 forms** — you need the sub's EIN or SSN before you can file; collecting W-9s after the fact is painful
- **Misclassifying employees as subs** — the IRS looks at construction companies closely for this; get it wrong and you owe back payroll taxes

### Revenue Recognition and Tax Method

Construction companies have two options for recognizing revenue on long-term projects:

**Percentage-of-completion method:** Revenue is recognized as work progresses, based on the percentage of the contract completed. This spreads income across the life of the project and is required for larger companies under GAAP.

**Completed-contract method:** Revenue is recognized only when the project is complete. This can defer taxable income for contractors who qualify — but the rules on who qualifies changed with the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act. This is a decision for your CPA, not your software.

### Sales Tax on Materials

Most states exempt materials that are permanently installed into real property from sales tax — but the rules differ for service work vs. new construction, for commercial vs. residential, and for materials you purchase vs. materials you supply from inventory. Some states require you to collect sales tax on the full contract price; others on materials only.

Get this wrong and you're either overcharging customers (and creating a competitive disadvantage) or undercharging (and owing the state the difference out of pocket).

## Top Tax and Payroll Compliance Tools for Construction

### QuickBooks Online + Contractor Edition — Best Accounting Foundation for Tax Prep

**Pricing:** $30–200+/month depending on tier; QuickBooks Enterprise $1,400+/year

QuickBooks is the accounting backbone for most construction companies under $10M. It doesn't handle certified payroll or 1099 tracking automatically out of the box, but it's the platform your CPA works in, and the construction-specific editions (QuickBooks Desktop Contractor, QuickBooks Enterprise with Contractor) add job costing features that make tax time less painful.

The 1099 workflow in QuickBooks Online is genuinely useful: track vendor payments throughout the year, mark subcontractors as 1099 vendors, and generate 1099-NEC forms at year-end with one-click preparation. If you've been collecting W-9s and entering payments correctly, January filing takes hours instead of days.

QuickBooks Payroll (add-on, $45–125+/month) handles W-2 and 941 filing, payroll tax deposits, and workers' comp reporting. It doesn't handle certified payroll directly — that requires a separate tool or a certified payroll module.

**What it does well:**
- 1099-NEC tracking and year-end filing preparation — best in class for this workflow
- Job costing that maps to tax cost categories
- Integration with virtually every construction PM and payroll platform
- Your CPA already knows it — no learning curve on their end
- Payroll add-on handles standard payroll tax compliance (W-2, 941, state filings)
- Sales tax tracking and reporting by customer and location

**What it doesn't do well:**
- No native certified payroll (WH-347) generation — requires a separate tool or manual process
- Job costing setup requires configuration that many contractors skip
- Percentage-of-completion revenue recognition requires manual workarounds or a construction-specific accounting platform
- Not a substitute for a construction CPA on complex tax decisions

**Best for:** Construction companies under $10M as the accounting foundation for all tax compliance — 1099 tracking, payroll tax, and year-end prep.

## Decision Framework by Revenue and Compliance Exposure

**Under $1M, no prevailing wage work:** QuickBooks Simple Start or Essentials at $30–60/month. Track sub payments as 1099 vendors from day one. Collect W-9 before paying any sub.

**$1M–$5M, occasional prevailing wage:** QuickBooks + LCP Tracker when you win a public job. Use QuickBooks Payroll for standard W-2 employees and LCP Tracker just for the prevailing wage jobs that require certified payroll.

**$5M–$10M, multiple prevailing wage jobs:** QuickBooks + LCP Tracker as a permanent stack. Consider Foundation Software or Viewpoint as alternatives to QuickBooks at this scale if job costing complexity is creating issues.

**$10M+:** Evaluate Sage 300 CRE or Viewpoint Vista. At this revenue level, the percentage-of-completion accounting, AIA billing, and certified payroll functionality in a single platform delivers enough time savings to justify the implementation cost.

## The W-9 Problem Every Contractor Should Fix Now

The single most common 1099 compliance failure is simple: contractors don't collect W-9 forms from subs before paying them. At year-end, you need the sub's legal name, address, and EIN (or SSN) to file the 1099-NEC. If you don't have it, you have to chase the sub in January when they're not responsive, or file with incomplete information and risk a penalty.

**Fix it now:** Add W-9 collection to your subcontract onboarding process. No W-9 on file = no payment issued. QuickBooks lets you flag vendors as 1099-required and track whether a W-9 has been received. Do this before the next sub starts work, not before the January deadline.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is certified payroll and when is it required?**
Certified payroll is a weekly payroll report (federal Form WH-347) required on federally funded projects and most state-funded public projects under Davis-Bacon Act and state prevailing wage laws. It documents that every worker was paid at least the prevailing wage for their trade classification. It's required for every week any worker is on the job — even a partial week — and must be submitted to the contracting agency within a specific timeframe.

**Does QuickBooks handle certified payroll for construction?**
Not natively. QuickBooks handles standard W-2 payroll, 941 filings, and 1099-NEC preparation well, but it doesn't generate the WH-347 certified payroll report. For certified payroll, you need a separate tool like LCP Tracker or B2W Compliance, or a construction-specific accounting platform like Sage 300 CRE that includes a certified payroll module.

**How do I track 1099s for subcontractors in QuickBooks?**
Set up each subcontractor as a vendor in QuickBooks and mark them as a 1099 vendor. Collect their W-9 before issuing the first payment. QuickBooks tracks all payments to 1099-marked vendors throughout the year. At year-end, go to the 1099 wizard to review payments, correct any issues, and generate 1099-NEC forms for e-filing. The process takes a few hours if the data has been entered correctly throughout the year.

**Is sales tax charged on construction contracts?**
It depends on the state and project type. Most states treat construction contractors as the end consumer of materials — you pay sales tax when you buy materials, and you don't charge sales tax on your contract price. Some states (notably Washington, New Mexico, and Hawaii) have different rules. Service work vs. new construction is often treated differently. Get a definitive answer from a construction CPA licensed in your state — the penalty for getting this wrong is paying the state the uncollected tax out of your margin.

**What is the percentage-of-completion method and do I have to use it?**
Percentage-of-completion recognizes revenue as work progresses based on project completion percentage. Under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (2017), contractors with average annual gross receipts under $26 million (2024 threshold, indexed for inflation) can use the completed-contract method, which defers revenue recognition until project completion. The choice has significant tax timing implications — consult a construction CPA before making the decision.

**¿Qué software es mejor para la nómina certificada y los impuestos en proyectos de construcción pública en Estados Unidos?**
Para nómina certificada (Formulario WH-347) en proyectos con salario prevalente, LCP Tracker ($150-500+/mes) es la opción más utilizada: se integra con QuickBooks y genera los reportes semanales automáticamente. Para empresas con ingresos superiores a $10M, Sage 300 CRE incluye módulos de nómina certificada junto con contabilidad de costos de trabajo y facturación AIA. Para el seguimiento de 1099 para subcontratistas, QuickBooks es la herramienta estándar — configure cada subcontratista como proveedor con requisito de 1099 desde el primer pago.
