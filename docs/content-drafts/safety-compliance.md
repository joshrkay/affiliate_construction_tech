# Best Construction Safety Compliance Software for 2025

An OSHA willful violation costs up to $156,259 per incident as of 2024. A serious violation runs up to $15,625. The difference between those numbers and a simple citation often comes down to documentation: did you have a written program, did you conduct training, did you inspect the worksite, and can you prove all three when the compliance officer shows up?

Paper sign-in sheets, handwritten inspection logs, and filing cabinet incident reports don't disappear during an audit — they just can't be found. Construction safety software doesn't make your job safer. It makes your documentation defensible.

This guide compares the top construction safety compliance tools, with honest notes on who each one fits and what it actually takes to get your crew using it.

## What Construction Safety Software Actually Does

### Three Separate Problems, Often Confused

Safety compliance software covers three distinct functions that get lumped together:

**Inspection and observation tools** — digital checklists for daily site inspections, equipment inspections, scaffold checks, crane inspections. The output is a timestamped, photo-documented record that the inspection happened, who conducted it, and what was found.

**Incident management** — near-miss reporting, incident investigations, corrective action tracking. OSHA requires written incident records for employers with 10+ employees. Software makes this faster and ensures the required fields get filled in.

**Training records** — toolbox talk documentation, safety orientation sign-offs, certification tracking (OSHA 10/30, fall protection, confined space). If you can't produce the training record, the training didn't happen from a liability standpoint.

The platforms below differ in which of these three they do well.

### The Documentation Gap That Costs Contractors

Most construction companies conduct safety inspections, run toolbox talks, and investigate incidents. The problem is documentation: the inspection happened but the form is in a truck, the toolbox talk happened but nobody signed in, the incident report was written on paper and filed in a box in 2019.

When an OSHA inspection happens or a lawsuit is filed, you're reconstructing records from memory. Digital safety software creates a timestamped paper trail automatically — photos, GPS location, employee signatures, corrective actions, and follow-through.

## Getting Your Team to Actually Use It

The biggest failure mode in construction safety software isn't the platform — it's adoption. Field supervisors who've been conducting inspections on paper for 15 years don't naturally switch to a mobile app without a reason.

**What works:**
- **Start with one inspection type.** Don't roll out every form at once. Start with daily site inspections — high frequency, visible to everyone, and easy to compare against paper.
- **Make it part of the sign-off process.** Completed inspection in iAuditor = project manager signs off on daily log. Incomplete inspection = follow-up call. One month of this creates the habit.
- **Show supervisors their own data.** iAuditor's analytics show each supervisor their completion rate. When a super sees their number, behavior changes.
- **Don't add forms without removing paper.** If paper forms still exist alongside digital, people use paper. Remove the paper.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What OSHA records are required for construction contractors?**
OSHA requires employers with 10 or more employees to maintain an OSHA 300 log (injury and illness log), OSHA 300A (annual summary), and OSHA 301 (incident reports). These must be retained for five years. Procore Safety includes OSHA 300 log management. iAuditor handles inspection documentation but not the OSHA 300 log directly.

**Do I need safety software if I have fewer than 10 employees?**
The OSHA 300 log requirement applies at 10+ employees, but the obligation to provide a safe workplace and document training applies regardless of company size. For companies under 10 people, iAuditor's free tier covers inspection documentation and toolbox talk records without cost.

**Can safety software reduce my workers' compensation premiums?**
Some insurers offer experience modification rate (EMR) credits for documented safety programs — inspections, training records, incident investigation procedures. The documentation that safety software creates is the same documentation your broker needs to argue for a better rate. Results vary by insurer and state.

**How do I track OSHA 10 and OSHA 30 certifications for my crew?**
Assignar has the most complete certification tracking — you can log each worker's OSHA certifications, expiration dates, and required courses, then set alerts before certificates expire. Procore Safety has basic credential tracking. iAuditor doesn't manage worker certifications natively.

**What's the best safety software for a roofing contractor?**
iAuditor with a fall protection inspection template is the starting point for most roofing contractors. The fall protection audit templates are pre-built, the mobile UX works well on rooftops, and the automatic PDF reports document that pre-work inspections happened. For roofing contractors with 20+ employees, adding Assignar for worker certification tracking handles the OSHA 1926.502 compliance documentation.

**¿Cuál es el mejor software de seguridad para contratistas de construcción en Estados Unidos?**
Para contratistas con menos de 50 empleados, iAuditor ($24-45/usuario/mes, con nivel gratuito disponible) es la mejor opción por su facilidad de uso y biblioteca de plantillas OSHA preconfiguradas. Para contratistas generales que ya usan Procore, Procore Safety integra la gestión de seguridad con la gestión de proyectos sin costo adicional significativo. Assignar es la mejor opción para subcontratistas con grandes fuerzas laborales que necesitan rastrear certificaciones individuales de trabajadores.
