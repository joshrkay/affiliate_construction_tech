## What Is Construction Time Tracking Software?

Construction time tracking software lets your crew clock in and out from their phones or a job site tablet, automatically assigns those hours to the right job and cost code, and feeds the data into your payroll and job costing system — without anyone transcribing paper timesheets.

The difference from a generic time clock: it knows about jobs, not just shifts. When your electrician clocks in, he picks the job he's working and the task (rough-in, trim, service call). That data flows to your job cost report automatically.

According to CFMA's 2024 Financial Benchmarker report, labor represents 38–52% of total project cost for most construction trades. It's your biggest expense — and for most contractors, it's the least accurately tracked.

The core benefits:

- **Accurate labor cost per job** — know what you actually spent on labor for every project, not just a lump sum
- **Payroll without the paper shuffle** — hours go from the field to payroll in minutes, not hours of manual entry
- **Real-time labor burn** — see if a job is running over on labor while you can still do something about it
- **GPS verification** — optional but valuable: confirm your crew is actually at the job site when they clock in

Contractors who switch from paper timesheets to dedicated time tracking apps report saving 3–5 hours per week on payroll processing alone, plus catching 8–15% labor cost discrepancies that were previously invisible.

## The Real Cost of Paper Timesheets

Paper timesheets feel free. They're not.

**Payroll processing time.** Your office manager spends 4–6 hours every pay period collecting timesheets, deciphering handwriting, entering data into payroll, and chasing down missing submissions. At $20–30/hour for admin time, that's $160–$360 every two weeks — $4,160–$9,360 per year, just to process payroll.

**Timesheet fraud.** A 2023 American Payroll Association study found that buddy punching (one employee clocking in for another) costs businesses an average of 2.2% of gross payroll. On a $1M payroll, that's $22,000 per year in wages paid for hours not worked. GPS-verified clock-ins eliminate this.

**Unbillable time miscategorized.** When your crew writes "Miller job" on Monday's timesheet and "Johnson job" on Tuesday's, but the timesheet arrives Friday with everything on one line — that's 8 hours you can't allocate correctly. Over a month of 3 active jobs, your labor cost per job is a guess at best.

**Delayed job costing.** Paper timesheets take 5–10 days to get from the field into your accounting system. By the time you see that the Riverside job is 20% over on labor, you're already committed to the next 3 weeks of work. With digital time tracking, you see it in 24 hours.

A GC running $1.5M in annual revenue with 8–12 workers is typically losing $15,000–$35,000 per year to payroll processing overhead, timesheet inaccuracies, and misallocated labor costs — all of which disappear with a $100–$200/month time tracking system.

## Construction Time Tracking vs. Generic Apps

Not all time tracking apps work for construction. Here's the difference:

| Feature | Generic Time Tracker (e.g., Toggl) | Construction Time Tracker |
|---------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Job/project assignment | Basic project tags | Cost codes, job IDs, phases |
| Clock-in method | App only | App, GPS, QR code, kiosk |
| GPS verification | Sometimes | Standard, with geofencing |
| Payroll integration | Limited | QuickBooks, ADP, Gusto native |
| Overtime rules | Basic | Prevailing wage, union rules, state OT |
| Crew management | Individual only | Foreman can clock in whole crew |
| Job costing output | None | Direct to job cost reports |

**When a generic app is fine:**

- You have 1–2 employees on a single job site at a time
- You're a solo operator tracking your own billable hours
- You don't need to allocate labor to job cost codes

**When you need construction-specific software:**

- Multiple crews on multiple sites simultaneously
- You need labor costs allocated by job for job costing or billing
- You have prevailing wage, union, or certified payroll requirements
- Your foreman clocks in a crew of 6, not just himself

## Key Features to Compare in Construction Time Tracking Software

### 1. GPS Clock-In and Geofencing

What it is: Workers clock in from their phone and the app records their GPS coordinates. Geofencing lets you set a radius around the job site — workers can only clock in when they're physically there.

Why it matters: "I was on site" is the most common excuse for disputed hours. GPS verification ends that conversation. For contractors managing multiple sites across a city, knowing which crew is where is operational intelligence, not just payroll data.

Contractor example: You're running 4 jobs across the metro area. It's Monday at 7:15 AM and you want to know who's on site and who's not. With GPS clock-ins, you open the app and see it in 30 seconds. Without it, you're calling foremen and waiting.

### 2. Foreman Mode (Crew Clock-In)

What it is: A foreman can clock in all 6 members of his crew at once from a single device, assigning them to the job and task they're starting.

Why it matters: If individual workers have to clock in themselves, compliance drops fast. Some workers forget, some don't want to use their phone, some are on sites with no cell signal. Foreman mode solves all three — one person manages clock-in for the whole crew.

### 3. Cost Code and Phase Tracking

What it is: When workers clock in, they pick not just the job but the specific task or phase — rough framing, electrical rough-in, insulation, finish carpentry. That detail flows into your job cost report.

Why it matters: Knowing that a job used 320 labor hours is less useful than knowing 180 went to framing, 85 to mechanical rough-in, and 55 to finish work. That breakdown is how you build accurate estimates for the next similar job.

### 4. Payroll Integration

What it is: Hours automatically sync to your payroll system — QuickBooks, Gusto, ADP, or others — with no re-entry.

Why it matters: Double data entry is where errors happen. Your payroll system should pull hours from your time tracking app, not require someone to re-type them every two weeks.

### 5. Overtime and Compliance Rules

What it is: Automatic flagging and calculation of overtime based on your state's rules — daily OT (California's 8-hour rule), weekly OT, double time, prevailing wage rates by trade.

Why it matters: Overtime miscalculations are the most common payroll lawsuit trigger for contractors. If you're working in states with daily overtime rules or on public projects with prevailing wage requirements, this isn't optional.

## How to Choose Construction Time Tracking Software

Evaluate options in this order:

**1. Payroll integration first.** If you're on QuickBooks, pick a tool that syncs natively. Switching your payroll system to accommodate a time tracking app is backwards. Check what integrations each tool supports before anything else.

**2. GPS requirements.** Do you need GPS clock-in? If your crews work a single job site or are mostly office-based subs, GPS is nice-to-have. If you're running 5 mobile crews across a region, GPS verification is essential.

**3. Crew size and foreman mode.** If your foremen manage crews of 4+, foreman clock-in mode saves significant friction. If your workers are all solo operators or work independently, individual clock-in is fine.

**4. Cost code complexity.** Are you allocating labor to 5 cost codes or 50? Simple jobs need simple tracking. Complex commercial projects with detailed cost code requirements need software that handles that structure.

**5. Mobile reliability.** Your crew will lose cell signal on job sites. Does the app work offline and sync when signal returns? Test this before committing — it's a deal-breaker for remote site work.

**You DON'T need dedicated field time tracking software if:**

- You have fewer than 3 workers and one job site
- Your bookkeeper handles payroll weekly and it takes under 2 hours
- You're not tracking labor by job cost code

**If you're moving from paper timesheets:** Set a hard cutoff date. Run parallel for 2 weeks — paper and app simultaneously — so you can verify the numbers match before ditching the paper. The biggest adoption challenge is getting older foremen comfortable with the app. A 20-minute group training session beats 6 months of individual troubleshooting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is construction time tracking software?

Construction time tracking software lets field workers clock in and out from their phones or a job site device, assigns those hours to specific jobs and cost codes, and syncs with payroll automatically. Unlike generic time clocks, it's built for multi-job environments where labor costs need to be allocated to individual projects for job costing and billing.

### Do I need time tracking software if I already use QuickBooks?

QuickBooks doesn't have field-ready time tracking built in — workers can't GPS clock in from a phone. QuickBooks Time (TSheets) adds that capability for $30–55/month and integrates natively with your QBO subscription. If you're already on QuickBooks, QBT is usually the path of least resistance.

### How much does construction time tracking software cost?

Prices range from $15/month (FreshBooks for solo operators) to $300+/month (Deputy for large crews with complex scheduling). Most contractors with 5–15 workers spend $50–120/month for a full-featured time tracking tool with GPS and payroll integration.

### What's the difference between time tracking and crew scheduling software?

Time tracking records when workers are on site and what they're working on. Crew scheduling plans who goes where and when. Some tools (Deputy, Procore) do both. Others focus on one. If you already have scheduling handled in a spreadsheet or another app, a dedicated time tracking tool is usually more cost-effective than switching to a combined platform.

### How does GPS time tracking work on construction sites with poor cell signal?

Most construction time tracking apps work offline — workers clock in without cell service, and the data syncs automatically when they're back in range. GPS coordinates are captured at clock-in time, so the location is still verified even if the sync happens later. Before committing to any tool, confirm offline mode is included in the plan you're evaluating.

### Which contractors benefit most from field time tracking software?

Contractors with 5+ workers across multiple job sites see the clearest ROI. The combination of GPS verification, automatic payroll sync, and job-level labor reporting pays for itself fastest for shops running $500K+ in annual revenue with crews that move between projects. Solo operators and very small shops doing under $300K often don't need more than a simple app or FreshBooks-style time tracking.

### Can I track prevailing wage and certified payroll with these tools?

Deputy and QuickBooks Time both support prevailing wage rates and certified payroll reporting. If you work on public projects or government contracts, confirm prevailing wage support before choosing a tool — not all time tracking apps handle the complex rate structures or reporting requirements. Procore has certified payroll support for customers on qualifying plans.

## Preguntas Frecuentes — Software de Seguimiento de Tiempo en Campo

### ¿Qué es el software de seguimiento de tiempo en construcción?

El software de seguimiento de tiempo de campo permite que los trabajadores registren su entrada y salida desde su teléfono, asigna esas horas a obras y códigos de costo específicos, y sincroniza con la nómina automáticamente. A diferencia de los relojes genéricos, está diseñado para entornos con múltiples obras donde los costos de mano de obra deben asignarse por proyecto.

### ¿Cuánto cuesta el software de seguimiento de tiempo para contratistas?

Los precios van desde $15/mes (FreshBooks para operadores individuales) hasta $300+/mes (Deputy para cuadrillas grandes con programación compleja). La mayoría de los contratistas con 5–15 trabajadores gastan $50–120/mes por una herramienta completa con GPS e integración de nómina.

### ¿Funciona el registro de tiempo con GPS en obras sin señal de celular?

La mayoría de las aplicaciones funcionan sin conexión — los trabajadores registran su entrada sin señal, y los datos se sincronizan automáticamente cuando recuperan cobertura. Las coordenadas GPS se capturan en el momento del registro, por lo que la ubicación queda verificada incluso si la sincronización ocurre después.
