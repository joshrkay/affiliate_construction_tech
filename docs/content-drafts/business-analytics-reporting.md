# Best Business Analytics Software for Construction Companies (2025)

Most construction companies have the data. Job costs in QuickBooks or Procore, labor hours in the time tracking app, bid history in the estimating tool, accounts receivable in accounting. The problem is it lives in four different systems and the only report that combines it is a spreadsheet the controller updates every Friday — already a week stale by Monday morning.

Business analytics tools pull that data into dashboards that answer the questions that matter: which project types are actually profitable, which project managers consistently overrun their budgets, what's the average days-to-collect on invoices, and where your bid-to-win ratio is falling below target.

Without that visibility, you're making million-dollar decisions about which projects to pursue and which PMs to promote based on gut feeling and month-old numbers.

---

## The Construction KPIs That Separate Profitable Companies from the Rest

Before evaluating software, know which numbers you're trying to track. The KPIs that construction companies with strong margins monitor consistently:

**Project profitability:**
- Gross margin by project type (commercial vs. residential, new construction vs. renovation)
- Estimated vs. actual cost variance by phase and cost code
- Change order capture rate — what percentage of scope changes get billed vs. absorbed

**Operational performance:**
- Revenue per field worker (total revenue ÷ field headcount)
- Labor efficiency ratio (budgeted labor hours vs. actual hours, by project)
- Equipment utilization rate for owned equipment

**Sales and pipeline:**
- Bid-to-win ratio by project type, size, and customer type
- Average bid preparation time vs. win rate (are you spending the most time on bids you win?)
- Pipeline value by project stage

**Financial health:**
- Days sales outstanding (DSO) — average days from invoice to payment
- Backlog by month and project type
- Cash flow forecast accuracy — how close your projections are to actuals

Most construction companies track some of these. The ones that track all of them systematically — with current data, not month-old spreadsheets — make better project selection and pricing decisions.

## Top Business Analytics Tools for Construction

### Procore Analytics — Best for GCs Running Projects in Procore

**Pricing:** Add-on to Procore platform; typically $5,000–15,000+/year depending on company size

Procore Analytics is a business intelligence add-on that turns Procore's project data into cross-project dashboards. If you're already managing projects in Procore, Analytics connects to that data without an export or integration — it reads directly from your live project records.

The value is cross-project insight. Standard Procore reporting shows you what's happening on a specific project. Analytics shows you what's happening across all projects: which project managers have the highest cost variance, which subcontractors have the most RFIs, which project types have the widest gap between estimated and actual labor costs.

The pre-built dashboard library covers the most common construction analytics needs without configuration: project financial summary, change order analysis, RFI volume and response time, subcontractor performance, and safety incident trends. For a GC with 10+ active projects in Procore, these dashboards surface patterns that individual project reports hide.

**What it does well:**
- Cross-project analysis — patterns visible across your entire portfolio, not just individual jobs
- Pre-built dashboards for finance, operations, safety, and subcontractor performance
- Live data — no export or refresh delay; dashboards reflect current Procore state
- PM performance benchmarking — compare cost variance, schedule adherence across PMs
- Subcontractor scorecards — RFI volume, punch list completion, schedule compliance
- No additional data infrastructure required — runs on top of existing Procore data

**What it doesn't do well:**
- Only works with Procore data — doesn't pull from QuickBooks, payroll, or other platforms
- Add-on cost on top of an already expensive Procore subscription
- Pre-built dashboards cover most but not all use cases — custom dashboards require Procore's BI tools or an export
- Not useful if Procore data quality is poor — analytics is only as good as what PMs enter

**Best for:** Commercial GCs with 10+ employees already on Procore who want cross-project visibility without building a separate analytics infrastructure.

### Sage Intelligence — Best for Contractors Using Sage 300 or Sage 100

**Pricing:** Included with some Sage tiers; $1,500–3,000+/year as an add-on

Sage Intelligence is the reporting and analytics layer built for Sage accounting platforms — Sage 300 CRE, Sage 100 Contractor, and Sage Intacct Construction. It pulls financial and job cost data from Sage and generates reports in a familiar Excel-based format, which means your controller or CFO doesn't have to learn a new interface.

The construction-specific report library is Sage Intelligence's strongest feature: pre-built reports for WIP (work-in-progress) schedules, bonding capacity analysis, job cost variance, and AIA billing summaries. For commercial GCs who need these reports for bank and bonding submissions, having them generated automatically from Sage data instead of assembled manually saves hours per report cycle.

The Excel integration works both ways: reports pull live Sage data into Excel, where your team can apply their own formatting, add commentary, and distribute to project owners or lenders without reformatting.

**What it does well:**
- Pre-built WIP schedule, bonding analysis, and job cost reports for Sage users
- Excel-based output that finance teams are already comfortable with
- Live Sage data in familiar spreadsheet format — no export/import cycle
- Construction-specific financial reports for bank and bonding submissions
- Lower cost than standalone BI platforms if you're already in the Sage ecosystem

**What it doesn't do well:**
- Only works with Sage platforms — no value outside the Sage ecosystem
- Excel-based approach limits interactive dashboard capability
- Cross-project operational analytics (PM performance, RFI trends) are weaker than Procore Analytics
- Requires Sage data quality — reports are only as clean as what's in Sage

**Best for:** Mid-market GCs and specialty contractors on Sage 300 CRE or Sage 100 Contractor who need WIP schedules, bonding reports, and job cost analytics without a separate BI platform.

### Microsoft Power BI — Best for Companies That Want Custom Dashboards Across All Systems

**Pricing:** $10–20/user/month (Power BI Pro); Power BI Premium from $4,995/month

Power BI is Microsoft's business intelligence platform — not construction-specific, but the most flexible analytics tool available for companies that want dashboards combining data from multiple systems. Connect QuickBooks, Procore, your payroll platform, your CRM, and your time tracking app into a single dashboard that shows your complete business picture.

The construction use case: a Power BI dashboard that shows revenue by project type (from QuickBooks), labor efficiency by PM (from your time tracking app), bid-to-win ratio by month (from your estimating tool), and cash flow forecast (from accounting) — all on one screen, updated automatically.

The tradeoff is build time. Power BI requires someone who can connect data sources, build data models, and create visualizations. That's either an internal resource with BI skills or a consultant. For companies without that capability, Power BI becomes shelf-ware despite its power.

**What it does well:**
- Connects to virtually any data source — QuickBooks, Procore, Excel, SQL databases, APIs
- Most flexible dashboard builder in this comparison — build anything
- Pre-built construction template reports available from the Power BI marketplace
- Microsoft 365 integration — embed dashboards in Teams, SharePoint
- Affordable at $10–20/user/month vs. purpose-built construction analytics
- Handles data volumes that spreadsheets can't

**What it doesn't do well:**
- Requires BI skills to build and maintain — not plug-and-play for construction
- No construction-specific pre-built content without marketplace templates
- Initial build time is significant — expect 4–8 weeks for a meaningful dashboard set
- Data connection maintenance burden when source systems update their APIs
- Value depends entirely on data quality in source systems

**Best for:** Companies with 20+ employees and an internal resource (controller, IT, or operations analyst) with BI skills who want cross-system dashboards connecting all business data.

## Decision Framework

**Under $3M, one PM platform:** Start with the reporting built into your existing PM or accounting software. Procore's standard project reports, QuickBooks' P&L by job — use what you have before buying analytics. The data maturity problem is usually the bottleneck, not the tool.

**$3M–$10M on Procore:** Procore Analytics is the path of least resistance. Pre-built dashboards, live data, no integration work. If you're not consistently entering project data in Procore, fix that first — analytics doesn't improve bad data.

**$5M–$20M on Sage:** Sage Intelligence for WIP schedules, bonding reports, and job cost reporting. Add Power BI later if cross-system visibility becomes a priority.

**$10M+ with multiple data sources:** Power BI with a dedicated resource (internal or contracted) to build and maintain the dashboard set. The multi-system connection capability is only available here, and at $10M+ the ROI on knowing your numbers accurately justifies the build investment.

## The Data Quality Problem That Kills Analytics ROI

Analytics is only as good as the data it reads. The two data quality failures that make construction analytics useless:

**Inconsistent cost code use:** If your PMs use job costs inconsistently — some using material vs. labor codes correctly, others dumping everything into a miscellaneous code — your gross margin by phase analysis is garbage. Fix the input before buying the output tool.

**Lagged data entry:** If PMs batch-enter labor hours on Fridays and vendors process invoices on a two-week cycle, your "current" job cost report is actually last week's reality. For analytics to drive decisions, data needs to be entered within 24 hours of the work happening.

Most construction companies that buy analytics tools and don't get ROI have a data discipline problem, not a software problem. If your PMs won't enter data consistently in the tool you have, they won't enter it consistently in the new tool either.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What KPIs should a construction company track?**
The most valuable construction KPIs: gross margin by project type (which work is actually profitable), estimated vs. actual cost variance by PM (who runs over budget consistently), bid-to-win ratio (are you chasing the right work), days sales outstanding (how fast are you collecting), revenue per field worker (labor productivity), and backlog by month (forward-looking revenue visibility).

**Does Procore have analytics and reporting features?**
Procore has standard project-level reports included with all plans — budget summaries, commitment logs, change order logs. Procore Analytics is a separate paid add-on that enables cross-project analysis: PM performance comparison, portfolio-level financial dashboards, and subcontractor scorecards. The add-on costs significantly more than the base platform and is best justified at 10+ active projects.

**Can I use Power BI for construction reporting?**
Yes — Power BI connects to QuickBooks, Procore, Sage, and most other construction platforms via native connectors or the Power BI marketplace. Construction-specific templates are available. The practical limitation is that building useful dashboards requires BI skills. If you don't have someone internally who can build data models, budget for a consultant or choose a purpose-built construction tool instead.

**What is a WIP schedule and why does it matter?**
A Work-in-Progress (WIP) schedule is a financial report showing the status of all open contracts — revenue earned vs. billed, and whether projects are over- or under-billed relative to completion. Banks and bonding companies require WIP schedules for construction credit. Sage Intelligence generates these automatically from Sage accounting data. For GCs using QuickBooks, WIP schedules typically require manual spreadsheet work or a third-party tool.

**How do I measure project manager performance in construction?**
The most reliable PM performance metrics: budget variance (estimated vs. actual cost at project completion), schedule variance (planned vs. actual completion), change order rate (number and value of change orders as a percentage of contract value), and safety incident rate. Procore Analytics has built-in PM performance comparison dashboards. Without a PM platform, this requires manual compilation from project records.

**¿Cuál es el mejor software de análisis de negocios para empresas de construcción en Estados Unidos?**
Para contratistas generales que ya usan Procore, Procore Analytics ($5,000-15,000+/año adicional) ofrece paneles de análisis entre proyectos sin infraestructura adicional. Para contratistas que usan Sage 300 CRE o Sage 100, Sage Intelligence ($1,500-3,000+/año) genera cronogramas WIP, análisis de bonos y reportes de costos de trabajo automáticamente. Para empresas con $10M+ que necesitan combinar datos de múltiples sistemas, Microsoft Power BI ($10-20/usuario/mes) es la opción más flexible, aunque requiere recursos técnicos para configurar y mantener los paneles.
