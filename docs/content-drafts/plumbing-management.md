# Best Plumbing Business Management Software for 2025

The second truck roll is where plumbing companies lose money. A tech arrives, finds the water heater is a 50-gallon Bradford White but he's carrying parts for a 40-gallon Rheem, and now someone drives back to the supply house. That's $80–150 in labor, a 3-hour delay, and a customer who leaves a 2-star review.

The right plumbing software doesn't just schedule jobs — it tracks every fixture, appliance, and service history at every location so your tech walks in with the right parts and a complete picture of what they're walking into.

This guide compares the best plumbing business management tools, with honest notes on pricing, who each one fits, and where the real ROI comes from.

---

## What Plumbing Contractors Need That Generic Software Misses

### Service vs. Project: Two Different Workflows

Most plumbing companies run two businesses inside one: residential service (leak repairs, water heater replacements, drain cleans, emergency calls) and new construction or commercial work (rough-in, trim-out, commercial service contracts). These require different software capabilities.

**Residential service** needs dispatch optimization, flat-rate pricing, customer history, and same-day invoicing. A delayed invoice on a residential call is a collections problem.

**Commercial plumbing** needs job costing, subcontract coordination, backflow inspection documentation, and multi-location customer management. A missed backflow certification renewal creates liability.

Most field service platforms handle residential service well. Fewer handle commercial plumbing adequately.

### The Fixtures and Equipment History Problem

When a customer calls about a water heater problem, your dispatcher needs to know:
- Make, model, and serial number of the installed unit
- Installation date (warranty status)
- Last service date
- What was found and quoted on previous visits

Generic scheduling software stores this as free-text notes that never get filled in. The platforms built for plumbing treat equipment records as a first-class feature — every location has a documented equipment list that techs see before they leave the shop.

### Flat-Rate Pricing Enforcement

Plumbing work has predictable items (water heater replacement, faucet installation, wax ring replacement) and highly unpredictable ones (slab leak location, drain line scoping). Flat-rate pricing works for the predictable items. Diagnosis-based pricing works for the unpredictable ones.

The software problem: if your techs don't have a price book in their hands, they quote jobs from memory and get different answers. A drain clean should cost $189 at every truck, not $189 from one tech and $140 from another. Price book enforcement is where ServiceTitan pays for itself on volume shops.

## Choosing the Right Platform for Your Shop Size

**Solo operator (1 tech):** Jobber at $29/month. You need scheduling, invoicing, and payment. The savings over HouseCall Pro or ServiceTitan funds 2–3 targeted Google ads per month.

## What a Software Transition Actually Looks Like

**Customer database import** is the hardest part. If you have 2,000+ past customers across multiple systems or spreadsheets, plan 8–16 hours of data cleanup. Don't import dirty data — fix addresses, remove duplicate entries, and standardize naming before the migration.

**Equipment records** are almost never in the old system. Plan to build equipment records as techs visit locations post-go-live. Set a standard: every tech fills in equipment details before closing a job.

**Price book build** (ServiceTitan and HouseCall Pro): your 50 most common services need to be priced before go-live. Techs won't use a price book with 10 items; they'll revert to quoting from memory. Commit to building the price book before your launch date, not after.

**Common mistakes for plumbing contractors:**
- Going live in peak season — transition in January or July when call volume dips
- Not training techs on the mobile app before their first live job
- Skipping equipment record setup — the history feature is useless without it
- Underestimating the price book build time — budget 20–30 hours for a complete book

**Which platform is best for commercial plumbing?**
ServiceTitan handles commercial plumbing better than the alternatives — it supports multi-site customers, service contracts at the account level, and the job documentation that commercial clients expect. For contractors doing significant commercial work, Procore or Sage is more appropriate for the project management side. ServiceTitan covers commercial service work well; it doesn't replace a dedicated project management platform for new construction.

Yes — all three platforms allow material logging per job. ServiceTitan's inventory management is the most complete, including truck stock tracking. HouseCall Pro and Jobber handle per-job material logging but have limited inventory management for truck stock replenishment.

For a plumbing shop under $3M, HouseCall Pro or Jobber's QuickBooks connection will work without significant setup effort.
