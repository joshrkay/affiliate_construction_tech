# Housecall Pro vs Jobber: Complete Comparison

Housecall Pro and Jobber are both popular field service management platforms for small contractors. Here's how they compare to help you choose the right one.

## Quick Summary

| Factor | Housecall Pro | Jobber |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Price** | $49-$99/mo | $29-$249/mo |
| **Best For** | Small-mid biz | Small biz |
| **Ease** | Easy | Very easy |
| **Marketing** | Built-in | Add-on |
| **Photo Docs** | Yes | Yes |

## Pricing

### Housecall Pro
- Essentials: $49/user/month
- Professional: $79/user/month
- Enterprise: $99/user/month

### Jobber
- Core: $29/user/month
- Grow: $49/user/month
- Scale: $249/user/month

**Winner:** Jobber for very small teams, Housecall Pro for feature set

## Features

### Scheduling
Both offer drag-drop scheduling, customer notifications, routing.

### Invoicing
Both offer professional invoices, online payments, recurring billing.

### Marketing
- **Housecall Pro:** Built-in marketing tools
- **Jobber:** Marketing add-on costs extra

### Photo Documentation
- **Housecall Pro:** Strong photo features
- **Jobber:** Basic photo capture

### Client Portal
Both offer online booking.

**Winner:** Housecall Pro for marketing and photos

## Best For

### Choose Housecall Pro If:
- You want marketing built-in
- Photo documentation is important
- You need professional features but not enterprise
- 5-15 technicians
- Residential focus

### Choose Jobber If:
- Budget is primary concern
- You have 1-5 technicians
- Very simple needs
- You prefer the simplest option
- Just need basics

## Verdict

For most small contractors, **Housecall Pro offers better value** at $79/user with built-in marketing and strong photo features.

**Jobber is better** for the smallest teams on tightest budgets.
