# Jobber Review: Field Service Software for Small Electrical Contractors

Jobber is a field service management platform designed specifically for small to mid-size service businesses—including electrical contractors. With pricing starting at just $29/user/month, it's one of the most accessible options for electricians just starting to digitize their operations.

## What Jobber Does

Jobber handles the essentials of field service: scheduling, dispatch, client management, invoicing, and quoting. It's designed to be easy to set up and use, with a focus on small teams who need professional tools without enterprise complexity.

**Who it's for:** Small electrical contractors (1-10 employees), solo electricians looking to grow, service businesses needing professional tools at budget prices.

## Key Features

### Scheduling & Dispatch
- Drag-and-drop calendar
- Technician availability tracking
- Client notifications (appointment reminders)
- Route views for the day

### Client Management
- Contact database
- Service history tracking
- Client portal for online booking
- Notes and documents per client

### Quoting & Invoicing
- Create professional quotes in minutes
- Convert quotes to jobs with one click
- Online invoice acceptance
- Recurring invoicing for maintenance contracts

### Mobile App
Jobber's mobile app lets technicians:
- View their schedule
- Capture photos
- Collect signatures
- Process payments on-site
- Access client history

### Payments
- Accept credit cards, debit cards, ACH
- Automatic payment processing
- Payment links for online invoices
- Integration with QuickBooks

## Pros

- **Affordable** - starts at $29/user/month
- **Easy to use** - minimal training required
- **Mobile app** - full functionality in the field
- **Client portal** - online booking saves phone time
- **Transparent pricing** - no "contact sales" runaround
- **Quick setup** - days, not months
- **80+ integrations** - QuickBooks, Stripe, Mailchimp, etc.

## Cons

- **Limited advanced features** - no complex job costing
- **Less commercial focus** - better for residential/service
- **Reporting is basic** - limited analytics compared to ServiceTitan
- **Scaling challenges** - companies over 20 techs may outgrow it
- **Marketing not included** - needs separate tools
- **Customization limited** - fewer workflow options

## Use Cases

### Case 1: Solo Electrician
A solo electrician uses Jobber to professionalize his business. He sends quotes from the field, captures signatures on his tablet, and gets paid immediately. The $49/month cost pays for itself in reduced administrative time.

### Case 2: Small Electrical Service Business
A 4-technician electrical company uses Jobber to schedule jobs, track time, and send invoices. They particularly love the client portal where residential customers book appointments online.

### Case 3: Growing Electrical Contractor
An electrical contractor growing from 2 to 8 technicians uses Jobber to standardize processes. The onboarding of new technicians is faster because the software is intuitive.

## Verdict

Jobber is the sweet spot for small electrical contractors who need professional tools without the complexity or cost of enterprise software. It's easy to set up, easy to use, and covers all the basics.

**Best for:** Small electrical contractors (1-10 employees), solo electricians, service-focused businesses, companies new to field service software.

**Skip if:** You need advanced job costing, have 20+ technicians, need commercial-grade features, or want everything (including marketing) in one platform.
