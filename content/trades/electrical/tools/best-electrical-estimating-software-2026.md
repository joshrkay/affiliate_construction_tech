## Togal.AI: Best for AI-Powered Takeoff

### Overview
Togal.AI uses computer vision and AI to automatically detect and measure electrical components—conduits, fixtures, panels, wiring—directly from PDF or raster plans. This can cut manual takeoff time by 60-80%.

### Pros
- **Fast takeoff**: AI detects components in seconds, not hours.
- **High accuracy**: Reduces missed items and measurement errors.
- **Easy learning curve**: Web-based interface works on any device.
- **Strong integrations**: Syncs with QuickBooks, Procore, and more.

### Cons
- **Requires quality plans**: Works best with clean, vector PDFs.
- **Higher price point**: May be overkill for very small shops.

### Contractor Verdict
> "Togal.AI cut our takeoff time in half. We've won 3 more bids this month alone." — *Mike R., Electrical contractor, 12 techs*

## Esticom: Best for Cloud-Based Estimating

### Overview
Esticom offers a cloud-based estimating platform with real-time collaboration, robust reporting, and strong accounting integrations.

### Pros
- **Real-time collaboration**: Multiple estimators can work on the same bid simultaneously.
- **Strong integrations**: QuickBooks, Xero, Procore, and more.
- **Scalable**: Works for small shops to enterprise teams.

### Cons
- **Less advanced AI**: Relies more on manual takeoff than Togal.AI.
- **Learning curve**: Slightly more complex than simpler tools.

### Contractor Verdict
> "Esticom gave us the visibility we needed into job costs. The integration with QuickBooks saves hours every week." — *Sarah L., Owner, 8-person electrical firm*

## STACK Takeoff: Best for Open Platform & Plugins

### Overview
STACK Takeoff provides a flexible, open platform with a marketplace of plugins and extensions, allowing you to customize your workflow.

### Pros
- **Plugin ecosystem**: Hundreds of extensions for specialized needs.
- **Flexible workflows**: Customizable assemblies and templates.
- **Cross-platform**: Works on any device with a browser.

### Cons
- **Requires setup time**: More configuration needed upfront.
- **Plugin costs**: Some extensions add to the base price.

### Contractor Verdict

## QuickBooks Enterprise: Best for Existing QuickBooks Users

### Overview
If you're already using QuickBooks for accounting, QuickBooks Enterprise offers advanced job costing, inventory management, and estimating features built in.

### Pros
- **Seamless QuickBooks integration**: No data sync needed.
- **Advanced job costing**: Track labor, materials, and overhead accurately.
- **Robust reporting**: Detailed profitability analysis.

### Cons
- **Requires QuickBooks**: Not ideal if you're on a different accounting platform.
- **Higher upfront cost**: Starting at $1,500/year.

### Contractor Verdict
> "Since we're already on QuickBooks, Enterprise was a natural fit. The job costing alone paid for itself in the first month." — *James P., Electrical contractor, 6 techs*

## PlanSwift: Best for Desktop Power Users

### Overview
PlanSwift is a desktop application known for its detailed measurement tools, extensive assemblies library, and robust reporting.

### Pros
- **Powerful measurement**: Drag-and-drop, point-and-click, and scripting.
- **Large assemblies library**: Pre-built assemblies for fast estimation.
- **Detailed reports**: Extensive reporting and export options.

### Cons
- **Desktop only**: Not accessible remotely without additional setup.
- **Steeper learning curve**: More complex than web-based alternatives.

### Contractor Verdict
> "PlanSwift is the gold standard for detailed takeoff. When I need precision, it's my go-to." — *Tom W., Senior estimator, 30-tech company*
