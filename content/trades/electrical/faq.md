# Electrical Contractor Software FAQ

Get answers to the most common questions about electrical contractor software.

## General Questions

### What is electrical contractor software?
Electrical contractor software helps electricians and electrical companies manage their business operations, including estimating, invoicing, scheduling, dispatch, customer management, and project tracking.

### Why do electrical contractors need software?
Software helps electrical contractors save time on manual tasks like takeoffs and invoicing, reduce errors, improve customer communication, and win more bids with accurate estimates.

### How much does electrical contractor software cost?
Prices range from $30-$250+ per user per month depending on features. Entry-level tools start around $30-50, mid-tier $75-150, and enterprise $200+.

### Can I try before buying?
Most vendors offer free trials (7-30 days). Take advantage of trials to test with your actual work.

## Estimating Software

### What is AI-powered takeoff?
AI-powered takeoff uses computer vision to automatically detect and measure electrical components (conduits, fixtures, panels) from PDF or raster plans, cutting manual measurement time by 60-80%.

### Which is better: cloud-based or desktop?
Cloud-based tools offer access anywhere, automatic updates, and easier collaboration. Desktop tools often provide more powerful features but require installation and aren't accessible remotely.

### Do estimating tools integrate with accounting software?
Yes—most modern estimating tools integrate with QuickBooks, Xero, Sage, and other accounting platforms. Always verify specific integrations before buying.

## Scheduling & Dispatch

### Can I track technicians in real-time?
Many field service platforms include GPS tracking to see technician locations in real-time, optimize routes, and provide customers with accurate ETAs.

### How do automated reminders work?
Automated reminders send text, email, or voice messages to customers before appointments to reduce no-shows. Most platforms let you customize timing and message content.

### Can I handle both residential and commercial jobs?
Yes—most electrical contractor software supports both residential and commercial workflows, though some specialize in one area.

## Pricing & Budget

### What's included in the price?
Most pricing is per-user-per-month and includes core features. Add-ons like advanced reporting, marketing automation, or additional integrations may cost extra.

### Are there hidden fees?
Watch for setup fees, training costs, premium support tiers, and charges for additional storage or users. Always ask for a full cost breakdown.

### Do prices increase?
Many vendors raise prices annually or when you add features. Ask about price history and any guarantees.

## Getting Started

### How long does implementation take?
Implementation typically takes 2-6 weeks depending on complexity and data migration needs. Most vendors provide onboarding support.

### Do I need training?
Most platforms offer free training via webinars, documentation, and videos. Some include dedicated onboarding with your purchase.

### Can I migrate data from my current system?
Most vendors help migrate data from legacy systems. Check what migration support is included.

## Security & Reliability

### Is my data safe?
Reputable platforms use encryption, cloud backups, and security certifications (SOC2, ISO 27001). Always ask about security practices.

### What happens if the vendor goes out of business?
Choose established vendors with strong financials. Ask about data export options so you can take your data if needed.
