# QuickBooks for Contractors Review: Accounting Software

QuickBooks is the most widely used accounting software for small businesses—including contractors. While not construction-specific, it offers contractor-focused features and integrations that make it the accounting backbone for thousands of construction companies.

## What QuickBooks Does

QuickBooks handles:
- Income and expenses
- Invoicing
- Job costing
- Payroll
- Tax preparation
- Financial reporting

**Who it's for:** Small to mid-size contractors, solo contractors, any construction business needing accounting.

## QuickBooks Options

### QuickBooks Online (QBO)
- Cloud-based
- Access anywhere
- Automatic updates
- Monthly subscription

### QuickBooks Desktop (QBD)
- Installed software
- One-time purchase
- More features
- Annual support fee

### QuickBooks Enterprise
- For larger businesses
- Advanced features
- More users
- Industry-specific

## Contractor Features

### Job Costing
- Track income by job
- Labor costs
- Materials costs
- Profitability by project
- Change order tracking

### Progress Invoicing
- Invoice by phase
- Retention tracking
- Milestone billing

### 100+ Integrations
- Construction software
- Field service tools
- Payroll
- Banking

## Pros

- **Industry standard** - widely used
- **Job costing** - contractor features
- **Integrations** - connects to everything
- **Familiar** - easy to find help
- **Scalable** - grows with you

## Cons

- **Not construction-specific** - requires workarounds
- **Learning curve** - complex
- **Expensive at scale** - costs add up
- **Support varies** - can be slow

## Verdict

QuickBooks is the default choice for contractor accounting. While not perfect for construction, its integrations and familiarity make it the best option for most small contractors.

**Best for:** Small to mid-size contractors, anyone needing accounting.

**Skip if:** You have very complex construction accounting needs (consider Foundation or Viewpoint).
